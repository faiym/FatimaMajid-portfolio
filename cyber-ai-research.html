Cyber/AI Research

US CYBERCOM | George Mason University

Project Summary

Over a 10-week intensive research period, I conducted applied research on AI-driven malware development and countermeasures in partnership with USCYBERCOM and George Mason University. This work explored adversarial tactics, infrastructure deployment by global threat actors, and nation-state cyber-attack strategies to support threat intelligence and allied collaboration.

Key Contributions

  • Analyzed adversarial use of AI in malware development, social engineering, and payload delivery.
  • Collaborated with technical and legal experts to evaluate dual-use AI technologies and their cyber policy implications.
  • Produced technical memos and briefings addressing data sovereignty, attribution, and counterintelligence strategy.
  • Mapped patterns of nation-state cyber-attacks for intelligence-informed defense planning.
  • Contributed to strategic recommendations for defense collaboration among the U.S., Japan, UK, Germany, and Canada.
